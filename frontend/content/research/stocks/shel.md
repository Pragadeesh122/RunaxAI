## Thesis

Own the **world's largest LNG trader and portfolio** at a **persistent ~12x P/E discount to US-listed majors**. The variant view: the market still prices Shell as a slow European oil major, but its earnings center of gravity has shifted to **Integrated Gas / LNG** — Q1 2026 delivered a **record 187 LNG cargoes** and a raised FY LNG outlook to **52–54 Mt**, with management explicitly centering near-term growth on natural gas/LNG against global LNG demand projected ~+60% by 2040. That makes SHEL a large-cap, diversified, **crude-insulated** way to play the same gas/LNG structural pull the desk is leaning into (Cheniere toller, EQT producer, gas-demand midstream) — but with a supermajor's downstream/trading cushion, a fortress balance sheet, and a relentless buyback. The discount *plus* the gas mix is the trade.

## Valuation basis

- **Relative:** ~12.3x trailing P/E and 1.3x P/B vs. US majors at materially higher multiples (CVX has screened well above 20x on depressed-cycle earnings) — a wide, structural discount for a comparable-or-better gas-weighted asset base and best-in-class LNG trading.
- **Shareholder-return engine:** **17th consecutive quarter of ≥$3B buybacks** (program reloaded), dividend raised ~5%, **~40–50% of CFFO returned** under a disciplined framework. ~3.7% yield + a high-single-digit buyback = a double-digit total cash-return yield that brackets a DCF-fair value above the current ADR.
- **Earnings power:** Q1 2026 adjusted earnings ~$6.9B (roughly doubled y/y on commodity recovery + LNG ramp) — the multiple does not reflect the through-cycle cash generation.

## Key risks (invalidators)

1. **Crude/refining drag.** Despite the gas tilt, a meaningful chunk of earnings is still oil-linked; sustained sub-$70 Brent + weak refining/chemical margins would pressure CFFO and the buyback pace.
2. **The discount persists (value trap).** European-major derating has been structural; the gap to US peers may not close without a catalyst, capping the re-rating leg.
3. **LNG normalization / FX.** LNG trading earnings are volatile quarter-to-quarter; a calmer gas market shrinks optimization gains. As an ADR, GBP weakness and UK tax/policy shifts are additional, non-operational swing factors.
