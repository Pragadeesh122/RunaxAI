## Thesis

Arqit is the **under-owned, second-order trade to the funded quantum-hardware push**: as government and enterprise compute spend advances, the quantum-safe **encryption/migration layer** inflects, and Arqit's SKA-Platform (symmetric key agreement) + NetworkSecure is a **capital-light SaaS** way to play it. The variant view is that a real **contract inflection** is now visible: H1 FY26 revenue of **$623K (+829% YoY** vs $67K) across **11 revenue-generating contracts** — versus 7 for all of FY25 — with management's own guide of **$2.48M FY26 / $7.8M FY27** revenue. Critically, Arqit has also launched an **Encryption Intelligence PQC-migration product** and executed its first contract on it, plus expanded collaborations with **Intel, 6WIND and RAD**, and a defence-sector partner **renewed and increased its contract ~90%**. The **~5.36M-share float** makes this a high-torque, venture-style expression of the post-quantum security theme — treat it as a small, asymmetric, speculative bet, not a core position.

## Key risks (invalidators)

1. **Extreme liquidity/volatility.** ~5.36M float and a $11.52–$62.00 52-wk range mean gap risk in both directions; **not sizeable without moving the tape**. This alone caps it at WATCH.
2. **Dilution risk (runway is real but finite).** Cash was **$28.9M at period-end, rising to $35.9M by 20 May 2026**; against **~$2.6M/month operating cost** that is **~13–14 months of runway** (into ~mid-2027). Not an imminent going-concern, but a capital raise into a 5.4M-share float would be **highly dilutive** — and the prior going-concern framing is the base-rate risk for sub-$3M-revenue frontier names.
3. **Standards risk (partially mitigated).** Arqit's symmetric-key approach is **non-standard vs NIST lattice PQC (ML-KEM/ML-DSA)** that buyers and competitors (incl. LAES) standardize on. The new **Encryption Intelligence PQC-migration product** is a meaningful hedge — Arqit now also sells *into* the NIST-PQC migration it was exposed to — but if procurement consolidates fully on NIST PQC, the SKA differentiation could be sidelined.
4. **Foreign issuer.** UK reporting cadence/disclosure (6-K filer) differs from US domestic filers — semi-annual rather than quarterly visibility, longer information gaps.
