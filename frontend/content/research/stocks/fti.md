## Thesis

TechnipFMC has effectively doubled over the trailing 52 weeks on a run of large subsea contract wins — Eni's Baleine Phase 3 (Côte d'Ivoire), Vår Energi's Ofelia/Gjøa Nord ($500M–$1B, one of its largest subsea awards in recent years), Azule Energy's Greater PAJ (Angola, $75M–$250M), and additional Equinor subsea work ($250M–$500M) — all landing within a roughly six-week window in June–July 2026. This is a genuine order-book story, not multiple expansion alone: iEPCI (integrated engineering-procurement-construction-installation) is FTI's structural differentiator versus pure-play subsea peers, and the contract cadence suggests offshore capex cycles remain healthy despite onshore U.S. shale capex discipline. The risk is that at a 22x forward P/E and within ~$7 of the all-time high, a meaningful amount of this order momentum is already reflected in the multiple — sell-side price targets diverge sharply by source ($75.81 stockanalysis.com vs. $69.64 MarketBeat consensus), and one of the more recent individual moves (Susquehanna, Jul 8) was a target *cut* ($90→$85) even while maintaining a positive rating, suggesting some sell-side caution creeping in near the highs. Constructive on the order-book durability, neutral-to-cautious on chasing the stock at current levels into the print.

## Valuation basis

- **Forward P/E 22.41x** is a premium multiple for an oilfield-services name — justified only if the subsea contract cadence (Baleine, Ofelia/Gjøa Nord, Greater PAJ, Equinor) continues at a similar pace through 2026–27; a slowdown would compress this quickly.
- **Analyst target dispersion is unusually wide:** median PT $80 vs. spot $70.53 (~13% implied upside) but with a $43–90 range depending on firm/date — Susquehanna cut to $85 (from $90) on Jul 8 even as RBC, Citigroup, Barclays, Goldman, and UBS all sit in the $76–87 band, mostly raised through May–June. Net skew is still bullish, but the one directional cut in the batch is notable given it came right at a fresh high.
- **Index-inclusion flow:** late-June addition to multiple Russell growth/value/defensive indices is a technical tailwind (forced buying), separate from fundamentals — worth discounting when reading the recent price strength.
- **Dividend yield 0.28%** is nominal — this is a capital-appreciation/order-book story, not an income name.

## Key risks (invalidators)

1. **Valuation-vs-momentum gap.** At 22x forward earnings and near an all-time high, FTI is priced for continued contract-win momentum; any quarter without a comparable award cadence would test whether the multiple can hold, especially with target dispersion this wide across the sell-side.
2. **Offshore capex cyclicality.** The current wave of subsea awards (Baleine, Ofelia/Gjøa Nord, Greater PAJ) reflects operators' offshore investment decisions made in a $75-80/bbl-ish price environment; a sustained crude selloff would eventually slow the FID (final investment decision) pipeline feeding FTI's order book, with a lag of several quarters.
3. **Execution/margin risk on rapid contract mobilization.** Simultaneously executing large iEPCI awards across West Africa (Côte d'Ivoire, Angola) and the North Sea raises operational-complexity and margin risk if project starts are staggered poorly or cost inflation isn't fully passed through in fixed-price components.
