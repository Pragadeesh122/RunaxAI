## Thesis

KMI is the **cheaper, cleaner (C-corp, no K-1) way to own the same data-center / LNG natural-gas demand theme** the desk already covers via WMB (watch, 32× P/E) and ET (watch, K-1 MLP). The variant view: WMB earns a premium ~32× multiple for the gas-demand story, while KMI offers the *same* secular thesis at **21× P/E and a 3.7% yield**, carrying the **largest pure-gas growth backlog in the group — ~$10B, ~90% natural gas, ~60% tied to power generation** — plus another ~$10B of potential projects (LNG feedgas, Mexico exports, industrial). Critically, KMI's cash flows are **throughput/demand-driven and largely oil-decoupled** — structurally insulated from the crude risk-premium now deflating across E&P/OFS (Brent ~$80, WTI ~$77 after an ~8.5% weekly drop on Hormuz de-escalation). That is exactly the dynamic that had midstream gas names closing *green* on 06-18 while the oil complex sold off.

## Valuation basis

- **Relative:** 21.3× trailing P/E and ~2.2× P/B — a clear discount to WMB (**32.1× P/E, 6.9× P/B**, verified 06-18) for a comparable secular-demand backlog and a superior, no-K-1 ownership structure. The discount is the trade: same theme, ~1/3 lower earnings multiple.
- **Income + growth:** ~3.7% yield, 9 straight years of dividend growth, covered by fee-based take-or-pay cash flows — income plus visible, backlog-driven EBITDA growth rather than a pure yield vehicle.
- **DCF sanity:** ~$10B contracted backlog at typical midstream build multiples adds material EBITDA across 2026–28 that a static 21× trailing multiple does not yet fully capitalize. FERC permitting has accelerated — Trident ($1.8B, under construction), Mississippi Crossing ($1.7B) and South System Expansion 4 ($3.5B) have scheduling orders; the data-center-serving Creekside Lateral (binding long-term contracts) targets in-service 4Q26.
