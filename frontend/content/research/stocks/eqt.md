## Thesis

EQT is the **lowest-cost, vertically-integrated US natural-gas pure-play** — the largest Appalachian producer, and the only major gas E&P that owns its own gathering/transmission backbone (post-Equitrans). It is trading like a distressed name — **~9.8x trailing P/E, 1.3x book, within ~6% of its 52-week low** — at the exact moment the gas setup is *improving*. The variant view: the tape is lumping EQT into the broad energy selloff driven by **crude** de-escalation (Brent sub-$74, WTI ~$71 on the Israel–Iran ceasefire + Hormuz reopening), but **EQT has near-zero crude exposure**. Its actual driver — Henry Hub — just firmed to a **2-week high (~$3.25/MMBtu)** on **record US LNG feedgas pull** (~17 Bcf/d capacity, Cheniere Corpus Christi Stage 3 Trains 5–6 ramping) plus warmer-weather demand. This is the cleanest operating-leverage expression of the desk's "gas/LNG insulated while crude rolls over" differential call: own the producer with the most torque to firming Henry Hub, bought at a washout multiple — not the toller.

## Valuation basis

- **Earnings / cash flow:** ~9.8x trailing P/E vs. a mid-cycle US gas E&P range of ~10–14x. The discount embeds depressed-gas pessimism the forward strip no longer supports.
- **FCF anchor:** management guides **~$3.5B 2026 free cash flow** (Q1 2026 was a record ~$1.8B FCF; adj. EBITDA ~$2.55B in Q1). $3.5B / $32.2B mkt cap = **~10.9% FCF yield**; EV/EBITDA ~5x on a ~$7–8B FY EBITDA run-rate — cheap for an integrated low-cost producer.
- **Balance sheet:** net debt cut to ~$5.7B, **leverage <1.0x** net debt/EBITDA, **Fitch upgrade to BBB**. Deleveraged, investment-grade — the downside is cushioned.
- **Cost structure:** FY26 per-unit operating cost guide ~$1.03–$1.21/Mcfe on 2,275–2,375 Bcfe volume — among the lowest corporate breakevens in US gas.

## Key risks (invalidators)

1. **Henry Hub rolls back over.** A mild winter setup, weather-driven demand miss, or an Appalachia/Haynesville production surge that pushes HH back toward ~$2.50 would gut the FCF and re-rate thesis — EQT is a price-taker on gas.
2. **LNG demand pull underdelivers.** Slippage/cost overruns in the Cheniere / Golden Pass / Plaquemines ramp, or feedgas maintenance, softens the structural demand leg.
3. **Multiple stays compressed / curtailment overhang.** If the market keeps paying gas E&Ps a trough multiple regardless of the strip, the cheapness doesn't close; continued strategic curtailments also cap realized volumes near-term.
