## Thesis

OXY's investment case rests on two pillars that both continued to develop this cycle: (1) an aggressive, well-evidenced debt-paydown program — $7.1B of principal debt repaid through May 5, 2026, cutting principal debt to $13.3B against a $10.0B target, funded substantially by the ~$8.0B after-tax proceeds from the OxyChem sale to Berkshire Hathaway (closed January 2, 2026, $9.7B cash, $3.1B after-tax gain) — and (2) Berkshire's continued large ownership stake, which functions as a quality signal even though the exact current percentage is genuinely unclear (SEC-confirmed Q1 2026 10-Q shows 26.64–26.9% common-only / 32.7% fully diluted with warrants, but secondary reporting describes further June 2026 open-market buying pushing the stake toward "almost 29%" without a corroborating fresh 13D/13G at the time of this pull). The July 8 Evercore ISI upgrade (Outperform, $58→$65) is the most concrete recent catalyst-confirming data point, explicitly tied to the deleveraging/strategy-shift narrative — though it stands in some tension with Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley both *cutting* targets in late June ($64→$60 Hold; $74→$68 Hold respectively). That split — one upgrade citing the strategy shift as a positive, two established banks trimming targets around the same week — suggests the debt-paydown story is real but not yet a unanimous sell-side view. CrownRock integration is described as complete with production above 1.2 MMboe/d and a credible 2026 well-count plan, providing operational continuity underneath the balance-sheet repair story. New CEO Richard Jackson (succeeding Vicki Hollub, effective June 1) adds a leadership-transition variable worth monitoring alongside the financial narrative.

## Key risks (invalidators)

1. **Berkshire ownership-percentage ambiguity.** The lack of a fresh 13D/13G confirming Berkshire's exact current stake (vs. the Q1 10-Q's 26.64–26.9%) means the market narrative of "Buffett keeps buying" is directionally true but imprecisely quantified — any read that treats a specific recent percentage (e.g., "29%") as confirmed should be treated with caution until a new filing lands.
2. **Split sell-side reaction to the strategy-shift narrative.** Evercore's upgrade (Jul 8) reads bullishly on the same week Goldman and Morgan Stanley both cut targets (late June) — this isn't a one-way re-rating yet, and the Aug 6 print is the next real test of which view is right.
3. **Trailing-P/E distortion risk for less careful readers.** The 70.7x trailing multiple (impairment-driven) could mislead a screen-based read of the stock as expensive when the forward multiple (9.54x) tells a very different story — and conversely, if further impairments hit in coming quarters, the "cheap on forward earnings" framing would need re-examination.
