## Thesis

The **value/contrarian counterweight** to my premium-medtech coverage ([ISRG](/research/isrg), [BSX](/research/bsx), [SYK](/research/syk)). The market prices MDT as a low-growth, conglomerate-discounted "device bond" — ~13–14x **forward** (non-GAAP) earnings versus ISRG/SYK in the mid-20s–40x — but two self-help levers are turning that the multiple does not reflect:

1. **The MiniMed (Diabetes) separation.** A two-step **IPO then split**, on track to complete **by CY-end 2026**, removes a lower-margin, capital-hungry unit and lets the cardio/neuro/surgical core re-rate on a cleaner, higher-growth mix. Interim dilution is small (~$0.01–0.02/qtr). MiniMed grew ~15% reported / ~8% organic into the separation — a standalone CGM/diabetes peer trades richer than MDT's blended multiple, so the spin surfaces sum-of-parts value RemainCo can re-rate on.
2. **Hugo robotic-assisted surgery** won **US FDA clearance in urology (Dec 2025)** — MDT is the first scaled medtech to field a US soft-tissue robot since Intuitive. Procedure volumes are growing multiples of the market rate with Touch Surgery installs ramping sequentially — a credible, under-modeled multi-year install-base story against ISRG's da Vinci franchise, alongside the **PFA / Catheter Ablation** ramp.

Management raised the **FY27 organic growth outlook to ~7%** with **6.7–8.5% non-GAAP EPS growth**. You're paid **~3.5%** (Dividend Aristocrat) to wait near 52-week lows while the catalysts de-conglomerate the multiple.

## Valuation basis

- **~14.7x trailing / ~13.7x forward P/E (non-GAAP).** FY26 actual non-GAAP EPS ran $1.26 + $1.36 + $1.36 + $1.55 = **$5.53**, so $81.49 / $5.53 ≈ **14.7x** trailing. FY27 consensus EPS ≈ **$5.96** (rev ≈ $38.6B) ⇒ $81.49 / $5.96 ≈ **13.7x** forward. (GAAP trailing P/E is 21.6x per live fundamentals — the gap is amortization/restructuring; the medtech group is valued on the non-GAAP line.)
- **Peer premium:** ISRG/SYK/BSX trade mid-20s–40x and the medtech group low-20s on forward earnings. A re-rate to even **~16–17x** on FY27 non-GAAP EPS ⇒ **$95–101**, ~**17–24% upside**, *before* any SOTP credit from the diabetes separation.
- **Sum-of-parts:** standalone MiniMed + a cleaner RemainCo should fetch more than the blended ~14–15x; the separation is the mechanism that surfaces it.
- **DCF sanity:** the ~3.5% yield + low-single-digit FCF growth roughly underwrites the current price on its own. Multiple expansion from the spin is **upside, not required** — that asymmetry is the point of the name.

## Key risks (invalidators)

1. **Value trap** — organic growth stays low-single-digit and the spin fails to re-rate RemainCo; you collect ~3.5% but the multiple stays stuck. MDT's multi-year record of mid-single-digit growth and uninspiring ROIC is the base-rate bear case.
2. **Hugo execution / competition** — slow US robotic adoption against ISRG's entrenched da Vinci installed base/ecosystem leaves robotics a perennial "almost" and removes the growth-optionality leg.
3. **Separation / FX / tariff drag** — a delayed or poorly-valued diabetes spin, or costs above guidance; large ex-US (Ireland-domiciled) footprint exposed to FX and tariff/policy headwinds.
