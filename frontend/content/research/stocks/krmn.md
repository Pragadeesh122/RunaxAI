## Thesis
The market cut KRMN **~62% from its January 2026 high ($118.38) to today's 52-week low ($43.49)**, lumping it into the SpaceX-IPO (June 12) "capital-siphon" derate that hit every listed space name. But Karman is **not** a pre-revenue space lottery ticket — it is a **profitable, ~51%-YoY-growth defense-hardware prime** producing payload-protection & deployment systems, aerodynamic interstage systems, and **propulsion systems for hypersonics, strategic missile defense, and tactical missiles**, with a **>$1B backlog** and FY26 revenue guided to **$720–735M**. The variant view: the selloff conflates a real-cash-flow missile-defense supplier with money-losing space speculatives. Karman is a direct beneficiary of the U.S. **hypersonics + "Golden Dome" missile-defense** spending wave and has disclosed **>$1B of contingent multi-year demand from four customers**. This is the frontier-tech book's *cash-flow diversifier*, picked up near trough sentiment rather than froth.

## Valuation basis
- **~8.2x EV/Sales** on the $727M FY26 revenue midpoint vs. closest pure-comp **KTOS (Kratos)** at ~$9.0B cap / ~7x sales — a modest premium *justified by faster growth* (51% YoY vs KTOS mid-teens). Growth-adjusted, KRMN screens comparable-to-cheap.
- GAAP **P/E ~205** is optically rich but depressed by D&A/amortization from its LBO/roll-up heritage; for a defense roll-up the cleaner anchors are EV/Sales and EV/EBITDA, where it reads reasonable given backlog coverage.
- Sell-side: 11 analysts, avg **Buy**, ~$105 12-mo target (~2x spot). I do **not** underwrite the full target — base case is mean-reversion of the sentiment-driven derate toward a **$60–75 fair zone** as Q2 confirms the growth/backlog story.

## Key risks (invalidators)
1. **Valuation gives no cushion** — at ~8x sales / ~200x GAAP earnings, any growth deceleration or a defense-budget continuing-resolution/sequester re-rates it hard.
2. **Lockup & sponsor supply overhang** — Karman IPO'd Feb-2025; sponsor (Trive Capital) secondary/lockup-expiry selling has been a real lid on the stock and can persist.
3. **Acquisition-driven growth & leverage** — Seemann + roll-up model raises integration risk, clouds the *organic* growth read, and lifts net debt with the cash deal.
