## Thesis

ONEOK carries the highest dividend yield (4.78%) of the three midstream names covered this cycle (vs. ET 6.8% distribution yield on the MLP structure, TRGP 1.83%), while backing it with real, quantified M&A synergy delivery: the Medallion Midstream acquisition ($2.6B cash, crude gathering with ~1,200 miles of pipe and ~1.3M bpd capacity) closed as part of the broader $5.9B GIP transaction, and management states combined synergies from Magellan Midstream (2023) + Medallion + EnLink integrations have **exceeded $500M annually** — a concrete, already-realized number rather than a forward promise. Q1 2026 results back this up: net income +12% y/y to $776M, adjusted EBITDA +13% y/y to ~$2.0B, with FY2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance raised to a ~$8.25B midpoint (~15% EPS growth / ~10% adjusted EBITDA growth guided for the year). The new JV with MPLX to build a 400,000 bpd Texas City LPG export terminal (targeted online early 2028) extends the growth runway and positions OKE to capture international LPG demand diversification away from Persian Gulf supply — a multi-year, not near-term, catalyst. Analyst sentiment is more mixed here than on ET or TRGP: consensus rating is genuinely split by source (Hold per MarketBeat at $91.75 target; Buy per stockanalysis.com at $95.38), and the July 8 Barclays move was a target *cut* ($90→$88, Equal Weight) even as most of the May–June cluster (Wells Fargo, BofA, Goldman, JPMorgan, Citigroup, Truist) raised targets — a data quality caveat: one scraped Wells Fargo figure ($79→$126) could not be independently corroborated and looks like an artifact; treat with caution.

## Key risks (invalidators)

1. **Consensus-rating ambiguity is unusually wide.** With MarketBeat (Hold) and stockanalysis.com (Buy) genuinely disagreeing on both rating and target by a meaningful margin, and one likely data artifact (Wells Fargo $126 figure) inflating the average on at least one source, OKE's "true" analyst consensus is harder to pin down than peers — a terminal cross-check is warranted before treating any single "Buy" or "Hold" tag as authoritative.
2. **Integration execution risk on three overlapping M&A deals.** Simultaneously integrating Magellan (2023), Medallion, and EnLink raises the usual multi-deal integration-complexity risk; the stated >$500M synergy figure needs to keep growing (not just hold) to justify the current multiple against slower-growing peers.
3. **Multi-year catalyst dependency (Texas City JV).** The MPLX LPG export terminal JV doesn't come online until early 2028 — a long-dated catalyst that offers limited near-term valuation support if the Q2 print or FY26 guidance disappoints in the interim.
