## What's driving this refresh — an unresolved, potentially large M&A situation

Williams has been in **advanced, unsigned talks since ~June 28, 2026** to acquire **Momentum Midstream** (a Haynesville-to-LNG-corridor gas gathering/pipeline network) from **EnCap Flatrock Midstream** for **approximately $5.5B** — one of the largest deals in company history if it closes at that size. As of **July 1**, reporting characterized the deal as **"announceable within a week"**; as of this **July 9** check, **no signed/definitive agreement has been confirmed**. The stock reportedly fell ~3.7% on the initial rumor given a reported **22–25x EBITDA multiple**, rich versus peer midstream deals typically done at **10–14x**. Separately, the board added two new independent directors effective July 1 (Billy Helms, former EOG Resources President/COO, and Robb Turner, energy-finance veteran/ex-ArcLight co-founder), expanding the board from 10 to 12 seats — a smaller, non-thesis-driving governance update.

## Thesis

WMB is a premium-multiple way to own the **Haynesville-to-Gulf-Coast LNG feedgas** demand theme — a structurally important corridor as US LNG export capacity continues to ramp. The Momentum Midstream deal, if it signs at anywhere near the reported terms, would materially deepen that Haynesville/LNG-corridor footprint, but at a multiple that the market has already signaled skepticism about (the ~3.7% rumor-driven drawdown). This is explicitly a **watch, not initiate** situation until the deal either signs (at which point terms/financing mix need underwriting) or lapses (in which case the standing toll-taker thesis reasserts on its own, at a cheaper implied entry after the rumor-driven pullback faded into this week's broader energy rally).

## Valuation basis

- **Relative:** WMB trades at a clear premium to KMI (per the desk's KMI writeup: ~32x vs. KMI's ~22x trailing P/E) for a comparable secular gas-demand thesis — the premium reflects WMB's Gulf Coast/LNG-corridor positioning and K-1-free corporate structure being priced in already, leaving less room for multiple expansion absent a clean growth catalyst.
- **Deal economics (if it signs):** a $5.5B deal at 22–25x EBITDA implies roughly $220–250M of incremental EBITDA — accretive only if funded without meaningfully diluting the balance sheet or if the Haynesville-to-LNG synergies (shared corridor infrastructure, contracted LNG feedgas demand) justify the premium versus the 10–14x peer-deal norm. Full underwrite pending signed terms and financing mix (debt/equity/JV structure).

## Catalyst (dateable)

- **Momentum Midstream deal — sign or lapse.** No hard date; was characterized as imminent as of Jul 1 and remains open as of Jul 9. This is the single highest-priority item to track for the next refresh.
- **Q2 2026 earnings** — date not yet announced (Q1 was reported May 4, 2026); estimate early-to-mid August based on cadence.

## Key risks (invalidators)

1. **Deal signs at a rich multiple with heavy debt funding.** A signed deal at 22–25x EBITDA funded mostly with debt would pressure leverage and could be read as overpaying for growth — the scenario the initial rumor-driven selloff already priced in partially.
2. **Deal lapses / falls apart.** Removes the growth catalyst this refresh is flagging, reverting WMB to a pure premium-multiple toll-taker with less near-term upside optionality.
3. **Premium multiple has limited room.** Already the most expensive name in the desk's gas-midstream comp set; any broader multiple compression in gas-demand names would hit WMB hardest in percentage terms.
