## Thesis

Targa is a genuine growth story within midstream — full-year 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance was raised to **$5.7–5.9B**, the quarterly dividend was raised 25% to $1.25/share ($5.00 annualized) in July, and the stock is up ~57–59% over the trailing 12 months on continued Permian NGL infrastructure buildout (Delaware Basin plant and pipeline expansion). Sell-side sentiment is broadly supportive: the July 9 JPMorgan raise ($291→$315) and a run of raises through May (Morgan Stanley to $331, Truist to $289, Mizuho to $300) point to genuine confidence in the growth trajectory, with only TD Cowen holding a Hold rating in the recent batch. That said, two things temper conviction at current levels: TRGP trades at a meaningfully higher EV/EBITDA (~14.9x) than peers in the same midstream comp set covered this cycle (ET 8.81x, OKE ~12.0x) — a premium that requires the growth guidance to keep delivering — and Simply Wall St has flagged insider selling with no offsetting insider purchases alongside the NGL-expansion narrative, a combination worth watching rather than dismissing. GuruFocus's GF Value model also flags the stock as overvalued (~$169.70 fair value vs. ~$273.81 price), though as with VLO, that model's trailing-multiple anchoring likely understates the case for a name executing a real growth ramp — the more relevant read is TRGP's premium-to-peer EV/EBITDA multiple, not the GF Value gap specifically.

## Valuation basis

- **EV/EBITDA ~14.9x** is the richest multiple among the midstream names covered this cycle (ET 8.81x, OKE ~12.0x) — TRGP is priced as the growth name in the group, consistent with its higher trailing-12-month return (+57–59% vs. ET's +11%), but leaving less room for error if the growth guidance disappoints.
- **FY2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance raised to $5.7–5.9B** — the concrete evidence behind the growth premium; a beat-and-raise cadence on this guidance is what would justify holding the multiple.
- **Dividend raised 25% to $1.25/qtr ($5.00 annualized)** in July — a meaningful capital-return signal alongside the growth capex, suggesting management sees the current EBITDA ramp as durable enough to support both.
- **Analyst consensus:** "Moderate Buy"/"Buy" (17-22 analysts depending on source, 17 Buy/2 Hold/0 Sell per MarketBeat), average target range **$274–$287**, roughly in line with-to-slightly-above spot — less of a gap than seen on some other names this cycle, suggesting the sell-side sees the stock as closer to fairly valued than deeply discounted at current levels.
- **Balance-sheet activity:** July 1 amendment/expansion of its receivables securitization facility (extended to July 2027, added $200M uncommitted line, $451M outstanding) — routine working-capital management, not a stress signal.

## Key risks (invalidators)

1. **Premium multiple vs. peer group.** At ~14.9x EV/EBITDA against ET's 8.81x and OKE's ~12.0x, TRGP needs the EBITDA growth guidance to keep being raised (not just met) to justify the gap; any guidance miss would compress the multiple toward peer levels, a larger percentage move than for cheaper-multiple midstream names.
2. **Insider selling without offsetting purchases.** Simply Wall St's flag of insider sales alongside no insider buying, set against an NGL-expansion bull narrative, is the kind of divergence that warrants monitoring for a change in insider sentiment as the growth story matures.
3. **Geopolitical-driven energy-infrastructure bid unwinding.** Some of the recent share strength has been attributed to broad energy-infrastructure buying tied to Iran-related geopolitical tensions; a de-escalation that reduces that sector-wide bid would remove a tailwind that isn't specific to Targa's own execution.
