## What changed this refresh (07-09) — the reason for the trim

**Genuine, thesis-relevant operational event:** a **rock-mass movement (rockfall) on the north wall of the Barnat open pit** at Canadian Malartic (Québec) occurred **July 1, 2026**. No injuries or equipment damage reported, but the company has since guided **FY2026 gold production to the low end of its 3.3–3.5M oz range**, with **H2 2026 production cut by an estimated 60,000–80,000 oz**, and flagged a possible **~150,000 oz/year** hit in **2027–2028** pending an ongoing geotechnical assessment of the pit wall. Several sell-side desks trimmed price targets following the disclosure. This is a single-asset, contained event (not a company-wide issue), but it directly cuts near-term production and introduces multi-year uncertainty at one of AEM's flagship assets.

## Thesis

AEM remains a **top-tier, low-political-risk (Canada-weighted) senior gold producer** riding an extraordinary gold tape — spot near **$4,110/oz**, a macro backdrop most of this coverage universe has never operated in. That tailwind is large enough to keep the name attractive even after the Barnat setback: at $4,100+/oz gold, even a 60–80k oz H2 production cut is a modest dent to consolidated revenue/FCF versus what the gold-price move alone is contributing. The Barnat incident is the reason conviction is medium rather than high this cycle — it's a real, not cosmetic, operational miss, and the 2027–2028 read-through (~150k oz/yr at risk) needs resolution via the ongoing geotechnical study before re-underwriting higher.

## Valuation basis

- **Gold-price leverage:** at ~$4,100/oz spot, AEM's all-in-sustaining-cost producer margins are historically wide — the single biggest valuation driver for any senior gold miner this cycle is simply the gold price itself, which is currently at a level that dwarfs typical AISC assumptions used in legacy models.
- **Production quality:** even at the low end of 3.3–3.5M oz guidance, AEM remains one of the larger, lower-jurisdictional-risk producers in the space — a valuation premium vs. peers in higher-risk jurisdictions is still warranted.
- **Caveat:** this page does not attempt a fresh P/NAV build this cycle; that re-underwrite should happen once the Barnat geotechnical assessment concludes and the Jul 29–30 print clarifies FY26 guidance precision.

1. **Geotechnical assessment worsens.** If the Barnat pit-wall study extends the production impact beyond the current 60–80k oz (H2 2026) / ~150k oz (2027–28) estimates, or requires a slower, costlier remediation, this becomes a larger multi-year overhang.
2. **Gold price reversal.** The thesis is heavily levered to a historically elevated gold price (~$4,100/oz); a sharp pullback (de-escalation of the geopolitical risk premium currently supporting gold) would compress margins and likely the multiple simultaneously.
3. **Read-across to other assets.** A single-mine geotechnical incident sometimes prompts broader portfolio safety reviews — watch for any read-through to AEM's other open-pit operations.
