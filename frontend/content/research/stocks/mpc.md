## Thesis

MPC's run to a fresh 52-week high on July 9 continues the same sector-wide refiner rally driving PSX and VLO (both also initiated this cycle) — a genuine crack-spread tailwind from extraordinary global refining-capacity outages (Russia, Persian Gulf, Mexico), not an MPC-specific catalyst. The July 8 +5.4% single-day move was explicitly tied to broad refiner-sector strength on falling gasoline inventories and reduced utilization, not company news. That makes this a "rising tide" story: MPC screens cheap on forward earnings (single-digit-to-low-teens forward P/E per most sources) against a trailing P/E of ~18x, and the last reported quarter beat badly (EPS $1.65 actual vs. $0.74 estimate) — but the same crack-spread reversion risk that applies to VLO and PSX applies here, and recent analyst action is genuinely split: TD Cowen and a Zacks downgrade to Hold trimmed views in mid-to-late June, while Goldman, Barclays, and Morgan Stanley raised targets in the same window. Recent addition to Russell Growth Indices has also drawn "is the upside already priced in" commentary. Net: ride the sector tailwind with the same discipline as VLO/PSX — this isn't a name-specific edge, and the risk/reward converges with the refiner group as a whole.
